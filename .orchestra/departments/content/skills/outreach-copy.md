# Outreach Copy — Content Skill

How to write emails, social posts, and newsletter submissions for IndieStack.

## Email Templates

### For makers (claim emails)
- Under 80 words
- From "Pat" not "Patrick"
- No subject lines with "opportunity", "partnership", or "collaboration" — gets ignored
- Hook: data-first ("agents recommended your tool X times" or "X GitHub stars but agents can't find you")
- CTA: one link, one action ("claim your listing")
- No upsell, no pitch

### Two template types
1. **Has MCP views (>0):** "AI agents recommended [tool] X times this month"
2. **Zero views:** "AI agents couldn't find [tool] — we fixed that. With Xk stars it should be showing up."

## Social Posts (Twitter/X)
- Under 280 characters
- No emojis, no hashtags
- Data-first — the number IS the hook
- Link to indiestack.ai/migrations
- No "check out our tool" language
- Let the data be interesting on its own

## Newsletter Submissions
- Console.dev: 3-4 sentence pitch, technical angle
- Changelog News: title + URL + 1 sentence description
- Show HN: technical opening comment, methodology first, limitations acknowledged

## Voice
- Casual, honest, from a uni student building something
- Never corporate or salesy
- "We" not "our platform"
- Acknowledge limitations ("our corpus skews frontend/JS")
- Data journalism tone for public content
